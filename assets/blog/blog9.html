09 Final Reflection Across the Full Codebase

Scope: entire development timeline | Focus: how the mechanics fit together

The important thing about this project is that it is not a collection of unrelated scripts. The blog should read like an engineering story: identity gates lead to lobby entry, lobby entry leads to session formation, role assignment shapes the player controller, controller state drives combat and objective interaction, and the objective systems feed into ritual progression and recovery.

The biggest technical pattern across the codebase is that I kept asking: who owns the truth for this state? In most cases, the answer is either the local client for input-only behavior or the master client for shared progression. That separation is what makes the game understandable and maintainable, and it is the part that most clearly reads as developer-authored work rather than generic blog filler.

SummaryProject-owned mechanics are now documented as a coherent gameplay stack: auth, lobby, spawn, controls, combat, health, resources, ritual pacing, and instrumentation. That is the real contribution.
